Frequency of tinnitus

Tinnitus (Latin tinnire, i.e. ringing) is a widespread phenomenon. Estimates speak of up to 50 million affected Austrians who have experienced ringing in their ears at least once in their lives. About 20 million Americans are likely to suffer from any type of tinnitus over a longer period of time.


Why tinnitus develops can have many causes such as:


  • constant or sudden exposure to noise
  • hearing loss, noise trauma
  • Viral diseases
  • Dizziness
  • Diseases of the cervical spine
  • Circulatory disorders of the head vessels
  • Middle ear infections
  • Impairments of the carotid artery or vessels in the ear
  • Heart valve diseases
  • Inflammations caused by rubella, toxoplasmosis or influenza
  • Medications (such as salicylic acid)
  • Hypothyroidism or hyperthyroidism, diabetes
  • Psychological overload such as stress


    • Tinnitus: Symptoms

    The ear noise as an acoustic perception in itself is not a disease, but a symptom. Comparable to pain, tinnitus is often a warning signal and indicates a malfunction, overload or disease in the ear.


    Tinnitus noises can be very quiet or "deafeningly" loud. The volume and also the type of sounds vary greatly from case to case and can also vary in the same person. The ringing in the ears can occur either for a short period of time, or in its chronic form over months or years, it can significantly reduce the quality of life and interfere with professional and personal life.


    The effects of tinnitus depend strongly on the subjective perception and assessment of the ringing in the ears. While some sufferers do not feel excessively disturbed by the noise and are hardly affected after a period of acclimatization, others suffer immensely from their constant companion. The permanent ringing in the ears can lead to sleep disturbances, depression or anxiety disorders.



    How does tinnitus occur?

    Tinnitus can have many causes - if we as physiotherapists want to help patients, we need to know the exact cause of the complaints. This is very important so that we cannot make false promises to people with tinnitus or say that physiotherapy will not help with certain causes. Because at the end of the day, it is crucial that people in health care professions are honest with patients and protect them from quackery if necessary.


    There are few clinics that have evaluated their diagnoses. An extensive study search led to only one publication in which diagnoses were given as a percentage - thus only the view of one group of experts is available, and this may differ according to experience, expertise or region. The values in the present study come from a research group in Portland (USA). Henry et al. (6) evaluated the diagnoses of 2,369 patients and came to the following conclusion:


    • 22 percent noise-induced tinnitus (for example, noisy work environment, explosion or blast trauma)
    • 17 percent head and neck injuries (head trauma, whiplash, concussion, fractures of the skull bones)
    • 13 percent other medical causes (other diseases, drugs / medications, stress, surgical intervention, CMD syndrome, barotrauma).
    • ten percent diseases in the head and neck area (inflammations / infections of the ear, cold / sinus infection, other ear diseases, acute hearing loss, allergies / hay fever).
    • No specific event could be identified in the remaining patients.

    Therapy of tinnitus

    In the case of acute tinnitus, treatment should be carried out quickly, but without rushing. Stress aggravates the symptoms. Treatment depends on the cause of the tinnitus.

    In the case of acute inner ear damage (e.g. hearing loss, middle ear infection, noise damage, circulatory disorder), the following therapies are available:


    • Infusion therapy: by means of blood circulation-promoting measures, the blood circulation situation is to be improved, e.g. infusion of common salt or cortisone.

    • Hyperbaric oxygen treatment (treatment in an oxygen overpressure chamber): This is usually used if the hearing loss or tinnitus does not improve within 2 to 3 weeks after administration of the infusion therapy and there is suspicion of a circulatory disorder.

    • Psychotherapy: If the tinnitus is exclusively the result of psychological factors (without organic involvement), infusion therapy and hyperbaric oxygen treatment are pointless. In this case, psychotherapeutic measures and/or stress reduction techniques are recommended.

    In the case of chronic tinnitus, the first step in treatment is an educational discussion to eliminate misinformation and prejudice. For example, tinnitus alone cannot be to blame for the deterioration of hearing.
